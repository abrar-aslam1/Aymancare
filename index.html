Dedicated primary care in Dallas.

A primary care provider is a healthcare professional that delivers ongoing healthcare services. Aside from diagnosing and treating acute and chronic illnesses, a primary care physician is often the first point of contact when pursuing healthcare options — and a steady partner through every stage of life.

What makes AymanCare different.

At AymanCare, your visits with Dr. Khan aren't rushed. We believe great care requires time — time to listen, time to ask questions, time to coordinate the right referrals. Our team uses data and technology to coordinate services so that when you come back, we already know you.

"The best primary care visit is the one that leaves you feeling heard, clear on next steps, and confident in who's walking the road with you."

Who we see.

We see patients of all ages — from pediatric check-ups to senior wellness exams, from newborn visits to Medicare-covered preventive care. Whether it's your first visit or your fiftieth, the goal is the same: the best possible health outcome for the person in front of us.

Life rarely sticks to a schedule. That's why AymanCare is set up to handle the unexpected — walk in for a sore throat, an ankle you rolled on the stairs, or the moment you realize you really do need to see someone today.

Most urgent care visits cost a fraction of an emergency room visit and are dramatically faster. For non-life-threatening conditions, AymanCare walk-in is the right first stop — board-certified care, standard clinic pricing, and a team who will refer you up if you need it.

More than just a cold.

Influenza is a contagious respiratory illness caused by flu viruses — and it can knock out even healthy adults for a week or more. At AymanCare, our board-certified physicians work closely with nurse practitioners to diagnose and treat the flu quickly and thoroughly.

Rapid testing & diagnosis.

We offer rapid flu testing with results in as little as 15 minutes. For many patients, same-visit diagnosis means same-visit treatment — including antivirals like Tamiflu when appropriate and clinically indicated.

"The flu isn't just an inconvenience. For the right patients, early diagnosis and treatment can change the course of the whole illness."

Recovery support.

Beyond medication, we help you plan: what to eat, when to call us if things worsen, and how to protect your family while you recover. For high-risk patients — young children, seniors, pregnancy, or compromised immune systems — we follow up closely.

Finding the cause.

Allergy symptoms can mimic many other conditions — and treating a symptom without understanding the trigger often fails. At AymanCare, we examine, test, and identify what's actually causing your reactions, whether it's seasonal pollen, your office air, a pet you love, or something in your diet.

Treatment that lasts.

Once we know the trigger, we build a plan — medication strategy, environmental changes, immunotherapy referrals when appropriate. The goal is lasting relief, not a lifetime of antihistamine roulette.

"Allergies don't have to be something you live around. The right plan can change your whole season."

For kids too.

Allergies often show up differently in children — chronic congestion that looks like a cold, eczema that won't quit, or stomach issues after certain foods. We work with parents to identify triggers early, when treatment is most effective.

More than a number.

Weight loss isn't just about a scale. It's about how you feel in your body, your energy, your sleep, your mood, and your long-term risk for conditions like diabetes, heart disease, and joint problems. Our program treats weight as one vital sign among many.

What the program looks like.

We start with a full health assessment — labs, history, lifestyle — and build a plan from there. That might include nutrition strategy, activity planning, medication support where appropriate, and ongoing check-ins so we can adjust the plan as your body changes.

"The best weight loss plan is the one you can actually live with for the rest of your life."

Built to last.

We're not interested in quick fixes that leave you back where you started in six months. The goal is a sustainable approach that builds your body's health and improves your habits — so you can enjoy life, not just fit into a number.

Two tests. One team.

We offer both rapid antigen and PCR molecular testing for COVID-19 at all four AymanCare locations. Whether you need a quick answer for peace of mind or a lab-verified result for travel or work, we have you covered — with results you can trust and a team that explains what they mean.

Rapid antigen testing.

Our rapid antigen test delivers results in 15–20 minutes using a simple nasal swab. It's ideal for symptomatic patients who need quick confirmation, families checking before gatherings, and employees screening before returning to work. If the result is positive, we can begin treatment guidance during the same visit.

"A fast result matters most when it changes what you do next. That's why we pair every positive rapid test with a same-visit care plan."

PCR molecular testing.

For situations that require gold-standard accuracy — international travel, employer mandates, surgical clearance — we offer PCR testing processed through a certified lab. Results are typically available within 24–48 hours, and we provide the documentation format most airlines and employers require.

Positive result? We're here.

A positive test doesn't have to mean navigating the next steps alone. Our physicians provide same-visit guidance on isolation protocols, antiviral medications like Paxlovid when clinically appropriate, symptom management, and monitoring plans — especially for patients who are older, immunocompromised, or pregnant.

How billing works at AymanCare.

We believe healthcare billing shouldn't be confusing. Here's how it works: you check in, we verify your insurance on the spot, you pay any copay at the time of service, and we handle the rest with your insurance company directly. If there's a remaining balance after your plan processes the claim, we'll send a clear, itemized statement.

Insurance verification.

Before your visit, our front desk can verify your insurance benefits so you know what to expect. We check your copay amount, deductible status, and whether any referrals are needed. If you'd like us to verify ahead of time, call us with your insurance details and we'll do the legwork before you arrive.

"We want you thinking about your health at your visit, not your bill. That's why we verify everything upfront."

Self-pay & uninsured patients.

No insurance? We offer transparent, competitive self-pay rates for all of our services. We accept cash, credit cards, HSA/FSA accounts, and can set up payment plans for larger balances. Our goal is to make sure cost never stands between you and the care you need.

Frequently asked questions.

Do I need a referral?

Most insurance plans do not require a referral for primary care or urgent care visits. If your plan is an HMO that requires referrals for specialist visits, we can help coordinate that during your appointment.

What if my insurance isn't listed?

We work with many plans beyond those listed above. Call us at (972) 288-9747 and we'll check your specific plan. We're continually adding new insurance partnerships.

Can I use my HSA or FSA?

Yes. We accept Health Savings Account (HSA) and Flexible Spending Account (FSA) cards for copays, deductibles, and self-pay visits.

Do you offer payment plans?

Yes. If you have a balance after insurance processing, we can arrange a payment plan that works for your budget. Just speak with our front desk team.
